How It Works

Five steps from first call to your keys

1

Free consultation

We talk about what you're looking for, your budget, and your timeline. I'll explain costs upfront (down payment, closing costs, monthly payment) so there are no surprises. If you need a lender recommendation, I have a few good ones.

2

Neighborhood strategy

Not every neighborhood fits every budget or lifestyle. I'll walk you through where your dollar goes furthest, what's trending up, and what to watch out for. This is where my appraisal background really helps.

3

Showings with an appraiser's eye

When we tour homes, I'm not just opening doors. I'm evaluating condition, spotting deferred maintenance, and comparing the asking price to what the home is actually worth based on recent sales data.

4

Offer with real data

Before you write an offer, I'll run comparable sales analysis (the same process banks use for appraisals) so you know exactly what the home should sell for. No emotional overbidding, no lowballing that wastes everyone's time.

5

Inspection through closing

I'll help you navigate the inspection, negotiate repairs, and keep everything on track through closing. You'll know what's happening at every step and why.

Usually not. Conventional loans go as low as 3% down. FHA loans are similar and more flexible with credit scores. The trade-off with less money down is private mortgage insurance (PMI), which adds to your monthly payment. A good lender will walk you through all the options. I have a few I trust and can recommend.

Plan on about 2% to 3% of the purchase price. That covers things like title insurance, the appraisal, prepaid property taxes, and homeowner's insurance. On a $300K home that's roughly $6,000 to $9,000 on top of your down payment. Your lender will give you an exact estimate early in the process.

Spring and summer have more inventory but more competition (and often higher prices). Fall and winter have fewer listings but also fewer buyers, which can mean less bidding pressure. The honest answer is: the best time is when you're financially ready and find the right home. Trying to time the market is a losing game for most people.

In most transactions, the seller pays both the listing agent's and buyer's agent's commissions. There are some situations where this can vary, and I'll always be upfront about how it works before we start looking at homes together.

Most agents estimate value by feel. I use the same comparable sales analysis that banks require for mortgage appraisals. That means when we're deciding what to offer, you're working with the same methodology the appraiser will use later. No surprises, no deals falling apart because the appraisal came in low.
