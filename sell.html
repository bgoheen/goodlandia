Most agents pick a list price based on gut feeling and what you want to hear. I use the same comparable sales analysis that appraisers and banks rely on. The result: your home is priced right from day one.

How It Works

Four steps from conversation to closing

1

Pricing consultation

We start with a real conversation about your goals, timeline, and what comes next. Then I run a full comparable sales analysis of your home, the same process I use as a licensed appraiser. You'll see the data and understand exactly how I arrive at a recommended price range. No inflated numbers to win your listing.

2

Prep that moves the needle

Not every project is worth the money. I'll walk through your home and give you a focused list of what to tackle and what to skip. Sometimes it's a deep clean and fresh paint. Sometimes it's even less than that. The goal is maximum return for minimum spend. If you need a handyperson, painter, or stager, I have people I trust.

3

Launch and market

Professional photos, a compelling listing description, and strategic timing. Your home goes live with a clear pricing strategy designed to generate strong interest. I'll keep you updated on showing feedback and any shifts in the market as they happen.

4

Offers through closing

When offers come in, I'll break down each one in plain terms so you can compare them clearly. I handle negotiations, inspection requests, and all the paperwork through closing day. You'll know what's happening at every step.

Common Questions

Selling a home in St. Paul

I run a comparable sales analysis using the same methodology I use as a licensed appraiser. That means I look at recent sales of similar homes in your area, adjust for differences in size, condition, and features, and arrive at a defensible price range. It's the same process banks use when deciding whether to approve a mortgage.

Spring and summer bring more buyers but also more competition from other sellers. Fall and winter have less inventory, which can work in your favor. The honest answer: the best time to list is when your home is ready and the timing works for your life. I can show you seasonal data for your specific neighborhood so you can decide with real numbers.

It depends on the neighborhood, price point, and condition, but homes in St. Paul that are priced correctly are typically selling within a couple of weeks right now. A home that sits on the market for 30+ days usually has a pricing problem, not a marketing problem. That's exactly why accurate pricing from day one matters so much.

Start with decluttering and a deep clean. Beyond that, I'll walk through your home and give you a specific list based on what buyers in your neighborhood expect. Not every project adds value. I've seen sellers spend $15,000 on a kitchen update that added $5,000 to the sale price. I'll be honest about what's worth doing and what's not.

The biggest cost is typically the agent commissions. Beyond that, you may have some prep expenses (paint, cleaning, minor repairs), and there are closing costs like title fees and state deed tax. I'll give you a clear net sheet early in the process so you know what you'll walk away with after everything is paid.

This is where my background really helps. Because I price homes using the same methodology appraisers use, there's a much lower chance of a surprise low appraisal. If the buyer's lender orders an appraisal and it comes in below the contract price, I know exactly how to respond because I speak that language every day.
